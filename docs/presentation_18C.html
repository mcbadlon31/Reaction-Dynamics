Transition-State Theory

Reaction Dynamics • Physical Chemistry

The Activated Complex

The Big Idea

Focus on the species at the top of the energy barrier: the Activated Complex ($C^\ddagger$).

$$A + B \underset{K^\ddagger}{\rightleftharpoons} C^\ddagger \xrightarrow{k^\ddagger} P$$

Assumes $C^\ddagger$ is in quasi-equilibrium with reactants.

The Eyring Equation

Deriving the rate constant from statistical mechanics:

$$k_r = \kappa \frac{k_B T}{h} e^{-\Delta^\ddagger G^\circ / RT}$$
$k_BT/h$: Universal Frequency Factor ($\approx 6 \times 10^{12}$ s$^{-1}$)
$\Delta^\ddagger G^\circ$: Gibbs Energy of Activation

Thermodynamic Formulation

Expanding $\Delta^\ddagger G^\circ = \Delta^\ddagger H^\circ - T\Delta^\ddagger S^\circ$:

$$k_r = \frac{k_B T}{h} e^{\Delta^\ddagger S^\circ / R} e^{-\Delta^\ddagger H^\circ / RT}$$

Enthalpy ($\Delta^\ddagger H^\circ$)

Corresponds to Activation Energy ($E_a$)

Entropy ($\Delta^\ddagger S^\circ$)

Corresponds to Pre-exponential Factor ($A$)

The Eyring Plot

Linearize the equation:

$$\ln\left(\frac{k}{T}\right) = -\frac{\Delta^\ddagger H^\circ}{R}\frac{1}{T} + \ln\left(\frac{k_B}{h}\right) + \frac{\Delta^\ddagger S^\circ}{R}$$

Activation Entropy ($\Delta^\ddagger S^\circ$)

$\Delta^\ddagger S^\circ < 0$ (Associative):
TS is more ordered (e.g., dimerization).
$\Delta^\ddagger S^\circ > 0$ (Dissociative):
TS is more disordered (e.g., bond breaking).

Kinetic Salt Effect

For ions in solution, ionic strength ($I$) affects the rate.

$$\log(k) = \log(k_0) + 2A z_A z_B \sqrt{I}$$

Select Charges:



Like charges ($z_A z_B > 0$): Rate $\uparrow$ with $I$
Opposite charges ($z_A z_B < 0$): Rate $\downarrow$ with $I$

Kinetic Isotope Effect (KIE)

Replacing H with D changes the rate.

$$k_H / k_D \approx 7$$

Due to Zero-Point Energy differences. C-D bond is stronger (lower ZPE) than C-H.

Quantum Tunneling

Particles can pass through the barrier, not just over it.

  • Important for light particles (H, e$^-$).
  • Significant at low T.
  • Leads to curved Arrhenius plots.
