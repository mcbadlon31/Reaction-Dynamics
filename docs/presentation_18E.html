Electron Transfer

Reaction Dynamics • Physical Chemistry

Marcus Theory & Biological Systems

Electron Transfer (ET)

The simplest class of reactions: just electron movement.

$$D + A \to D^+ + A^-$$
  • Biology: Photosynthesis, Respiration.
  • Chemistry: Redox, Batteries.
  • Marcus Theory: Nobel Prize 1992.

Franck-Condon Principle

Electrons move much faster than nuclei.

Key Consequence:
ET occurs at fixed nuclear geometry.
Nuclei must reorganize before the electron jumps.

Reorganization Energy ($\lambda$)

The energy cost to rearrange nuclei.

$$\lambda = \lambda_{\text{in}} + \lambda_{\text{out}}$$

Inner Sphere ($\lambda_{\text{in}}$)

Bond lengths and angles changes.

Outer Sphere ($\lambda_{\text{out}}$)

Solvent reorientation.

The Marcus Equation

Activation energy depends on driving force ($\Delta_r G^\circ$) and reorganization ($\lambda$):

$$\Delta G^\ddagger = \frac{(\Delta_r G^\circ + \lambda)^2}{4\lambda}$$

Rate constant:

$$k_{ET} \propto \exp\left(-\frac{\Delta G^\ddagger}{RT}\right)$$

Three Regimes of ET

  1. Normal Region:
    Rate increases with driving force.
  2. Barrierless:
    Maximum rate at $-\Delta_r G^\circ = \lambda$.
  3. Inverted Region:
    Rate decreases with driving force!

The Inverted Region

Counter-Intuitive!

Making a reaction more exergonic can slow it down.

Why? The surfaces intersect at high energy because the product well is "too deep".

Biological Use:
Nature uses the inverted region to prevent wasteful back-electron transfer in photosynthesis.

Distance Dependence

Electron must tunnel through the medium.

$$k_{ET} \propto \exp(-\beta r)$$

Rate drops exponentially with distance.
