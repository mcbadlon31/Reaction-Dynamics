Collision Density

The number of collisions per unit volume per unit time:

$$Z_{AB} = \sigma \left(\frac{8k_BT}{\pi \mu}\right)^{1/2} N_A^2 [A][B]$$

Where $\sigma$ is the collision cross-section and $\mu$ is the reduced mass.

The Steric Factor ($P$)

Not all collisions lead to reaction.

  • Orientation matters!
  • $P$ represents the fraction of collisions with the correct orientation.
  • Usually $P \ll 1$ for complex molecules.
  • "Harpoon Mechanism" can lead to $P > 1$.
